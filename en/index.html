WhichOne is made for choices that are too small for serious analysis but still manage to slow you down. What to eat, where to go, what to do, who goes first: put the options into a playful decision game and let the answer appear naturally.

01 Overview

Many decisions feel tiring not because the options are complex, but because they are almost equally fine.

WhichOne turns that small hesitation into a light decision ritual: choose a scene, let the black cat guide the process, reveal a result, and move on.

The app is designed for low-stakes everyday choices: what to eat, where to go, what to do, who goes first, or how a group should split. It keeps the decision quick while making the moment feel playful.

Decision Companion

For choices that are small, but still sticky.

When every answer is acceptable, WhichOne gives the user a clear next step instead of another blank moment.

02 Cat IP
Black Cat IP

One character carries the whole decision experience.

The black cat runs through WhichOne: topics, built-in options, mini games, team splitting, cards, coins, and result moments. That repeated presence makes the app feel like a companion, not just a utility.

Its mischievous expression softens hesitation. The app is essentially saying: stop overthinking, let the cat help you pick one.

03 Options
Built-in Options

Start from common questions: eat, go, do.

WhichOne prepares option sets for food, places, and activities. Users can quickly generate choices based on preference, then edit the options freely before starting a decision game.

Option Set 01

What to eat.

Food choices help users move past the most common daily question. The app can provide a useful starting list, while users can still add, delete, or rewrite options to match taste.

  • Quickly starts dinner, takeout, and group meal decisions.
  • Editable options keep the result aligned with real preference.
Option Set 02

Where to go.

When the plan has no destination yet, the app gives users a direction first: nearby places, short outings, weekend ideas, or travel candidates can all become decision options.

  • Useful for nearby plans, trips, and weekend outings.
  • The result suggests a direction while the user stays in control.
Option Set 03

What to do.

Leisure, chores, family time, and personal tasks can all be turned into a light draw. The generated list gives users momentum, and editing keeps it personal.

  • Good for free time, family activities, and small tasks.
  • Turns “anything is fine” into something executable.
04 Modes
Solo & Party

Two modes cover private decisions and group moments.

Solo mode keeps a personal decision short: pick a topic, edit options, reveal a result, and keep history for replay.

Party mode turns the decision into something shared: pick one person, play together around one device, or enter participants to split into teams.

05 Games
Game Library

A small library of polished decision games.

The app does not rely on one randomizer with different labels. Wheels, cards, capsules, coins, books, dice, and party tools give each choice a different rhythm, so repeat use still feels lively.

More Than Random

Play makes the result easier to accept.

Instead of dropping an answer instantly, WhichOne lets users watch a wheel slow down, flip a card, draw a capsule, scratch a result, or wait through a small reveal.

01

More games, less repetition

Different game forms keep frequent decisions from feeling like the same button press.

02

Gestures become part of the answer

Spinning, scratching, flipping, tapping, and multi-touch selection make the decision feel tactile.

03

A short wait gives the result weight

Anticipation makes a random answer feel more like a small event, especially in party mode.

06 Care
Visual & Accessibility

Bright color, clear feedback, and practical accessibility.

WhichOne uses a high-brightness yellow, strong black contrast, playful accent colors, haptic feedback, and large-type support so the experience stays readable, touchable, and easy to understand.

A warm yellow system with black contrast

Yellow keeps the app bright and light, while black anchors text, buttons, and the cat character. Accent colors support scenes and results without carrying meaning alone.

Haptic feedback

Confirmation, result reveals, and state changes use Apple device haptics to make feedback easier to perceive.

2x text size and large type

Important titles, options, buttons, and result feedback are designed to remain readable and tappable even when system text size is increased significantly.

Languages and device layouts

Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and English are supported. iPhone, iPad, portrait, and landscape layouts are considered for solo and group use.

Product Notes

A few boundaries.

WhichOne is for everyday lightweight decisions and group interaction. It is not for high-risk professional judgment.

Use boundary: Suitable for low-risk choices such as food, places, activities, who goes first, and grouping. Not intended for financial, medical, or legal decisions.

Random and playful: The app does not predict the future and does not include real-money betting, cash rewards, or gambling mechanics.

Data and purchase: User options, recent picks, and history are mainly stored on device. An optional in-app purchase removes ads.
