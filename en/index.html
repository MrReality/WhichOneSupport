For life's little decisions

Decide with a little luck.

WhichOne is made for choices that are too small for serious analysis but still manage to slow you down. What to eat, where to go, what to do, who goes first: put the options into a playful decision game and let the answer appear naturally.

01 Origin

Many decisions feel tiring not because the options are complex, but because they are almost equally fine.

WhichOne turns that small hesitation into a tiny ritual: tap, spin, scratch, reveal, and move on.

It is closer to a gentle decision companion than a productivity tool. It keeps randomness light, then adds visual, tactile, and haptic feedback so the process feels worth handing over.

Design Intent

Made for tiny everyday friction.

When everyone says “anything,” when the menu takes too long, or when a weekend plan stalls, WhichOne gives that moment a playful way forward.

02 Scenes
Everyday Scenes

Start with a real question, not a blank form.

People often open the app with a specific question already in mind. WhichOne prepares common scenarios so users can begin without inventing a list first.

Scene 01

Get dinner moving.

For dinner, takeout, or a meal with friends, the hard part is often that every option is acceptable. Built-in food choices lower the starting cost and can still be adjusted to taste.

  • Preset food options reduce blank-page work.
  • Useful for group meals, takeout, and weekday dinners.
Scene 02

Give an outing a direction.

When a weekend, trip, or quick outing has no clear destination, a random result does not replace planning; it simply gives the plan a first step.

  • Good for places, nearby activities, and short plans.
  • The result suggests a direction while the user stays in control.
Scene 03

Turn free time into a small draw.

Movies, walks, chores, games, and small personal plans can all become a light decision. Once the result appears, it is easier to act.

  • Suitable for leisure, family time, and personal tasks.
  • Turns “anything is fine” into something executable.
03 Game Modes
Game Modes

A random result deserves a playful process.

A plain answer can feel thin. WhichOne hides the result inside small games: answer book pages, coin flips, capsules, wheels, scratch cards, and other reveals make randomness visible, tactile, and expected.

Party mode turns the screen into a shared moment. Touch selection, bottle spinning, bomb cards, and capsule games let people wait for the result together instead of having one person announce it.

04 Visual
Visual Language

Bright, not loud. Cute, not childish.

The high-brightness yellow makes decision-making feel lighter. Black anchors text, buttons, and the cat character. Coral, green, and pink stay as measured accents for scenarios and feedback.

The black cat is not just decoration. It gives the app a companion-like personality: a little mischievous, as if saying “stop overthinking and try it.”

05 Craft
Interaction Craft

Delicate feedback gives the result weight.

Random apps can easily feel weightless. WhichOne tunes touch feedback, physical motion, and Apple haptics so each game has its own rhythm instead of feeling like the same button with a new skin.

01

Actions have anticipation and closure

Wheel slowdown, capsule drawing, and card flipping all create a short wait before the result lands.

02

Touch is more than tapping

Multi-touch selection, scratch cards, and card games make gestures part of the experience.

03

Haptics match key moments

Confirmation, result reveals, and state changes use Apple device haptics to make feedback easier to perceive.

06 Devices
Accessibility & Devices

Designed for real use: color, type, orientation, and devices.

WhichOne is not built for one ideal screen size. It considers iPhone and iPad, portrait and landscape, solo handheld use and people gathering around a device, while keeping color, type, and feedback clear for more users.

Color choices for color-weak users

Status is not communicated through color alone. Text, shapes, button forms, and contrast work together so key information remains understandable.

2x text size and large type

Important titles, options, buttons, and result feedback are designed to remain readable and tappable even when system text size is increased significantly.

Portrait, landscape, and iPad

Solo decisions feel natural on iPhone in portrait, while party play and group selection benefit from iPad or landscape layouts.

Languages

Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and English are supported so scenarios, options, and results are easy to understand.

Product Notes

A few boundaries.

WhichOne is for everyday lightweight decisions and group interaction. It is not for high-risk professional judgment.

Use boundary: Suitable for low-risk choices such as food, places, activities, who goes first, and grouping. Not intended for financial, medical, or legal decisions.

Random and playful: The app does not predict the future and does not include real-money betting, cash rewards, or gambling mechanics.

Data and purchase: User options, recent picks, and history are mainly stored on device. An optional in-app purchase removes ads.
